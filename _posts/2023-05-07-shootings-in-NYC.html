New York City is among the major metropolitan areas in the world, with a population above 8 million, not only known for its iconics landmarks, cultural richness and diversity but also for its high cost of living and criminality.

This study provides an in-depth analysis of the trends, patterns, and correlation with some indicators that might be associated with gun violence and specifically, shooting incidents, which have been a persistent problem in the city. The research is based on extensive data collection from NYC Open Data, together with statistical and visual analysis learned trhough the Social Data Analysis and Visualization course, which reveal important insights into the nature and extent of gun incidents in the city. The findings of the study have important implications for those who seek to address this pressing public safety issue.

The city of New York has implemented various strategies and initiatives to combat gun violence. Here are some of the ways that the city representatives have tried to fight against gun violence:

  • Gun Control Laws: New York City has some of the strictest gun control laws in the country. The city has implemented measures such as background checks, limits on gun purchases, and requirements for gun owners to register their firearms. These measures aim to prevent criminals and individuals with a history of violence or mental illness from obtaining firearms.[1]
  • Policing Strategies: The New York City Police Department (NYPD) has implemented various policing strategies to reduce gun violence, such as increasing police presence in high-crime areas, using data-driven approaches to identify and target gun violence hotspots, and conducting gun buyback programs.[2]
  • Community Outreach: City representatives have also focused on community outreach programs to address the root causes of gun violence, such as poverty, lack of education, and unemployment. These programs aim to provide opportunities for at-risk youth and promote positive behaviors.[3]
  • Partnerships: The city has also formed partnerships with community organizations, faith-based groups, and businesses to work together in addressing gun violence. These partnerships help to raise awareness, provide resources, and create a network of support for individuals affected by gun violence.[4]

Impact on school attendance

The first thing that came to our minds is that gun violence and shootings could have a significant impact on school attendance. When incidents occur in the vicinity of schools or within the community, parents may be hesitant to send their children to school out of concern for their safety. In some cases, schools may be closed or placed on lockdown as a precautionary measure, further disrupting students' attendance and academic progress. Additionally, students who are exposed to or directly affected by gun violence may experience trauma, anxiety, and fear, which can affect their ability to attend school and concentrate on their studies.[5]

We have created a variable -z-score- which allows us to identify when the attendance for a certain school was extraordinarily affected (the lower, the more unusual the attendance drop). Figure 4 shows the correlation between z-score and number of shootings in the same day as the shooting, while Figure 5, the attendance impact on the next working day. Logically, all weekend and other non-school days have been excluded in the analysis.

Figure 4: Dispersion chart of shooting location vs school attendance on the same day.
Figure 5: Dispersion chart of shooting location vs school attendance on the next day.
Although we acknowledge there are many factors that affect school attendance (weather, strikes, or others), we can see that the lowest z-score values occur when the distance from the shooting to the nearest school is also the lowest. In our analysis, this makes sense as parents could be scared from a possible shooting. Overall, the city representatives have taken a multi-faceted approach to combatting gun violence in New York City, addressing the issue from various angles to create a comprehensive strategy.
