Proposals and Projects

 U-  Urban Systems P-  Public Safety and Security G-  Games S-  Simulations V-  Visualisations

  • Violence against Women- U, P, S, G, V
    We submitted a proposal titled "Women on the streets: Unpacking Women's resilience to violence through Games, Visualisations and Simulations" to the IDRC competition "Safe and Inclusive Cities".
  • Smart Campus- U, S
    We submitted the proposal "Towards a decentralised architecture for a smart campus using multi-agent systems" to IIIT-B, which was approved in August. The project is divided into two phases, and we are now in the process of data collection (Phase 1).
  • Games for training- P, G
    The proposal "VR training system for operation and maintenance of an in-vessel robotic arm" submitted to the Institute of Plasma Research was accepted by the Board of Research in Fusion Science and Technology. The aim of the project is to train scientists and technicians to operate and maintain a robotic arm for the Steady State Superconducting Tokamak. We have started work on the project and are scheduled to visit IPR, Gandhinagar in October.
  • Understanding the institutional structure of the ICDS program- U
    We are working with Maraa, a Bangalore based NGO, to understand the institutional structure of the ICDS program and how the communities can be involved in the management of the Anganwadis. The effort focuses on looking into malnutrition in urban poor and understanding its consequences. Maps and infographics evolving out from this effort will be aimed at helping the communities in understanding malnutrition and its impact.
  • Visualising health guidelines- U, V, S
    We are working with the BBMP to develop information brochures for Dengue. We are also evaluating the use of the tool EventShop to identify epidemic outbreaks in Bangalore. EventShop is a tool developed by Prof. Ramesh Jain's group in the University of California, Irvine.
  • Namma Cycle Lanes- U, G
    Using Unity3D, we are trying to develop a single player awareness game about the use of cycle lanes (which were very recently introduced in a few areas in Bangalore) and whether or not the current implementation is sufficient.
